About Me

I am an early‑career researcher working at the intersection of machine learning and field robotics. After a top‑rank B.Tech. in Electrical Engineering (AMU). I joined the Erasmus Mundus Master in Intelligent Field Robotic Systems, splitting my time between the University of Girona and Eötvös Loránd University. My projects span sample‑efficient navigation, multi‑sensor SLAM, and vision‑based quality assessment for medical images.

My motivation is simple: turn rigorous AI into tools that matter. Whether it is a robot mapping post‑disaster rubble or a model guiding clinical decisions, I want the algorithms I build to reduce human effort and enlarge human impact.

I’m seeking collaborations with labs, startups and NGOs that share this goal. If you have a societal problem that intelligent robots or data‑driven models could help solve, let’s explore it together.

Recent News

  • Our paper “Fréchet Radiomic Distance (FRD): A Versatile Metric for Comparing Medical Imaging Datasets” has been submitted to Medical Image Analysis (MIA); a preliminary version is available on arXiv. FRD introduces a clinically interpretable metric based on radiomic features and shows improved performance over existing metrics in tasks such as out-of-domain detection, image translation and abnormality detection.
